## Reasons to Use a Microcontroller (MCU) over a Single-Board Computer:

* **Extreme Power Efficiency:** Microcontrollers are designed for low power consumption, allowing them to run for weeks, months, or even years on small batteries. In contrast, single-board computers like the Raspberry Pi 4 require significant power, often needing a 3A USB-C power supply.
* **Compact Physical Size:** MCUs are typically much smaller than single-board computers. Some microcontrollers are so tiny they can fit inside the dimple of a golf ball, making them ideal for embedded applications where space is highly constrained.
* **Real-Time Performance and Simplicity:** Because they do not run a resource-heavy desktop operating system, MCUs can use Real-Time Operating Systems (RTOS) or run "bare metal" code. This makes them better suited for specific tasks that require immediate, predictable responses to peripheral data.

## Reasons to Use a Single-Board Computer (SBC) over a Microcontroller:
* **High Processing Power and Memory:** SBCs feature CPUs with multiple cores running at Gigahertz (GHz) speeds, compared to the Megahertz (MHz) speeds typical of microcontrollers. They also offer Gigabytes (GB) of RAM, providing the memory necessary to handle large mounts of data that a microcontroller’s Kilobyte-range RAM cannot support.
* **Full Operating System and Software Flexibility:** SBCs run complete operating systems (such as Debian Linux), which allows users to utilize a wide range of programming languages, tools, and libraries. This makes them ideal for complex tasks like running machine learning models or managing high-resolution 4K video output.
* **Extensive Built-in Connectivity:** Unlike microcontrollers, which primarily rely on GPIO pins, SBCs come equipped with standard computer peripherals, including multiple USB 3.0 ports, Ethernet, and HDMI outputs, making them easier to integrate into traditional computing environments.
