ETC
1. How IoT works in ETC
  The system consists of three main components that connect in real time:
    Identification card (ePass/VETC): Affixed to the headlights or windshield of the vehicle, containing a unique identification number.
    Reader (RFI Reader): Installed at toll booths to identify the card as the vehicle passes (even at high speeds).
    Central processing system: Connected to the traffic/bank account to deduct money and control the automatic opening of the barrier.

2. Upsides
    Reduced traffic congestion: At gateways to Ho Chi Minh City, such as the Hanoi Highway toll station or the Ho Chi Minh City - Long Thanh - Dau Giay expressway, vehicles no longer have to stop to buy paper tickets, resulting in smoother traffic flow.
    Cost and time savings: Reduced fuel consumption due to the elimination of frequent vehicle stops and starts. It is estimated that this system saves society trillions of dong annually.
    Financial transparency: All transactions are recorded digitally, preventing revenue loss or fraud related to paper tickets.
    Environmental protection: Reduced harmful emissions from vehicles waiting at toll stations and minimized the printing of millions of paper tickets annually.

3. Downsides
    System technical errors: Sometimes the cameras fail to recognize license plates or RFID tags malfunction, preventing the barrier from opening and causing localized traffic disruptions.
    Payment difficulties: Users sometimes encounter errors when topping up their traffic accounts, as the system hasn't updated in time, resulting in being denied passage through the toll booth even with sufficient funds.
    Conversion costs: Although initial tag installation is usually free, maintaining and upgrading the infrastructure requires significant investment from BOT investors.

4. Privacy Considerations
  Privacy is a sensitive aspect in IoT mobility monitoring projects:
    Traffic data: The system stores information about the time and location of vehicle passage through toll stations. This raises concerns about the tracking of individual travel routes.
    Security measures: Data encryption: Operators (such as VETC, VDTC) commit to encrypting customer information and only providing it to authorities upon legal request (e.g., criminal investigation or traffic violations).
    Access control: Only authorized personnel have access to the historical travel database.
    Risks: If the system is subjected to a cyberattack, personal information and linked bank accounts could be compromised. Therefore, from 2025, Ho Chi Minh City has tightened cybersecurity standards for providers of this service.
